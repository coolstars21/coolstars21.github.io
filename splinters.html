COOL STARS 21

4-9 July 2022, Toulouse, France

The "Cambridge Workshops of Cool Stars, Stellar Systems and the Sun" are held biennially and have evolved to be the premier conference series for cool star research.

The conference will be held in hybrid format. Remote registration is still open until June 30th.

 

Splinter sessions

Splinter sessions are going to be held on Monday, Tuesday, and Thursday afternoon. There will be three splinter sessions in paralell. These sessions are organized separately from the main conference. The websites are linked from the splinter session title in the list below.

  • Monday 4th July
    • session 1: What’s Up With Spin Down?
      Marcel Agüeros et al.

    • session 2: Brown dwarfs beyond a quarter-century: what did we learn and what is missing?
      Nicolas Lodieu et al.

    • session 3: Solar and stellar coronal mass ejections
      Iulia Chifu et al.

  • Tuesday 5th July
    • session 4: Machine Learning for Cool Stars
      Thibault Merle et al.

    • session 5: Characterising stellar variability in the era of extreme radial velocity surveys of low-mass planets orbiting F-M stars
      Nadège Meunier et al.

    • session 6: Manifestations of star-planet interactions
      Antoine Strugarek et al.

  • Thursday 7th July
    • session 7: Accretion in young cool stars: connecting the star, the disk, and the planets
      Aurora Sicilia-Aguilar et al.

    • session 8: Towards a better understanding of cool star magnetised winds and mass-loss rates
      Victor See et al.

    • session 9: Gaia and Spitzer’s Neighbourhood Watch Program: From Stars to Brown Dwarfs to Planets
      Alexander Binks, Jackie Faherty
